About Me


TLDR -> You can view my CV here

Currently I am a first year CAPP candidate at the University of Chicago Harris School of Public Policy. I also work as a data scientist and occasionally as a data engineer for ConsiliumBots, an NGO that leverages economic theory and technology to make navigation in education markets easier for families and students.

Previously I was a Predoctoral Fellow (a synonym for research assistant) at Yale’s Tobin Center for Economic Policy for Christopher Neilson and John Eric Humphries where I developed tools to assist the research team and performed statistical data analysis.

I completed my B.A. in Economics at Southern Connecticut State University, graduating in December 2016. Throughout my undergraduate degree I worked at a small local company as a business analyst where I learned some of the pragmatic ruggedness that often comes handy in academic research.

My own interests lie within the intersection of the fields of education, public policy, computer science and technology.
